what it is

What

A 30-minute mobility flow that takes your wrists, elbows and shoulders through the loaded ranges a flare needs.

why it matters

Why

The flare lives in your wrists. Cold wrists snap; warm wrists float — the difference between feeling stuck and throwing your hips around.

how to do it

How

1Five minutes light cardio first — jump rope or shadow boxing.
2Wrist circles (30s each direction), then loaded wrist push-ups against a wall.
3Scapular circles + banded dislocates × 12.
4Hold a tabletop bridge for 3 × 30s — feel the shoulders lengthen.
cues to feel
  • No pinch in the wrist — only stretch.
  • Shoulders rotate cleanly past your ears.
  • You finish breathing through your nose.

re: why does this work?

cold wrist tendons resist the loaded supination a flare demands — you'd compensate at the elbow. the circles + banded dislocates flood the joint with synovial fluid, so by minute 8 your wrists move like they belong to someone else. that's the prep your hips need to swing freely.

re: what will I get wrong?

most people rush step 2 and skip the wall push-ups. that's the whole point. don't.
